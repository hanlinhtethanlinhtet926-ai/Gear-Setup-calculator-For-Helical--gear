⚙️1. Helical Gear Setup

Determine the correct change gears for cutting helical gears on manual milling machines.

Required Inputs

  • Desired Lead: The helix length required (mm).
  • Leadscrew Pitch: Distance spindle travels per revolution (mm).
  • Dividing Head Ratio: Usually 40:1 for standard machines.

Setup Logic

Gear Ratio = Desired Lead / (Leadscrew Pitch × Dividing Head Ratio)

🔧2. Gear Train Analysis

Analyze multi-stage speed reduction or increase systems.

Analysis Features

  • Two-Stage Ratios: Input teeth counts for Pinion/Gear pairs.
  • Transverse Geometry: Calculated from Pressure and Helix angles.
  • Efficiency: Automatic estimation based on 98.5% efficiency per stage.
💡 Tip: Leave optional second-stage fields blank to analyze a simple single-stage reduction.

3. Industrial Bolt Torque

Determine clamping force and tightening requirements for structural fasteners.

The Nut Factor (K)

Select the lubricant/coating to automatically set the K-factor:

  • Dry Steel: K = 0.20
  • Lubricated: K = 0.15
  • Zinc-Plated: K = 0.22
Torque (T) = K × Diameter (D) × Preload Force (F)
