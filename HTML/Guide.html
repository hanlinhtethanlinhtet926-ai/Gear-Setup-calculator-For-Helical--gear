⚙️ Quick Reference Guide

Master the Helical Gear Setup Calculator

🚀Getting Started

The Helical Gear Setup Calculator helps you determine the correct change gears for cutting helical gears on a manual milling machine with a dividing head.

What You Need to Know

  • Desired Lead: The pitch of the helix you want to cut (in mm)
  • Leadscrew Pitch: The pitch of your machine's leadscrew (typically 4-5 mm for metric machines)
  • Dividing Head Ratio: The gear ratio of your dividing head (usually 40:1)
💡 Tip: Most manual milling machines use standard specifications. Check your machine manual for exact values if unsure.

📋Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Enter the Desired Lead

Input the helix length you want to cut. This is measured in millimeters. Common values range from 30 mm to 100 mm.

Example: 56.0 mm

Step 2: Set Leadscrew Pitch

Enter the pitch of your machine's leadscrew. This is the distance the spindle travels for one complete revolution.

Example: 4.5 mm (typical for metric machines)

Step 3: Specify Dividing Head Ratio

Enter the gear ratio of your dividing head. This determines how many turns are needed for one complete spindle revolution.

Example: 40:1 (most common)

Step 4: Click Calculate

Press the "Calculate Gears" button to get your optimal gear combination.

📊Understanding the Results

Gear Set Output (A × B / C × D)

The calculator returns a combination of four gears:

  • A & B: Input gears (driver side)
  • C & D: Output gears (driven side)
Example output: 20 × 90 / 20 × 28
This means: Use a 20-tooth gear × 90-tooth gear on input, and 20-tooth × 28-tooth on output

Accuracy Metrics

Metric Meaning What's Good?
Actual Lead The exact pitch you'll achieve with selected gears Should match desired lead closely
Error % Deviation from desired lead as percentage Less than 1% is excellent; 0% is perfect
Machine Constant Internal calculation factor Reference value for your machine setup

🔢Formula Reference

The calculator uses the following mathematical relationship:

Machine Lead Constant = Leadscrew Pitch × Dividing Head Ratio

Gear Ratio = Desired Lead / Machine Constant

Actual Lead = Gear Ratio × Machine Constant

Standard Gear Sizes

The calculator can select from these standard gear tooth counts:

20, 28, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 55, 60, 65, 70, 75, 80, 90, 100

📚Common Examples

Example 1: Standard Setup

Input: Lead = 56.0 mm, Pitch = 4.5 mm, Ratio = 40
Output: 20 × 90 / 20 × 28
Error: 0.000% (Perfect match!)

Example 2: Tight Lead

Input: Lead = 30.0 mm, Pitch = 4.5 mm, Ratio = 40
Output: 50 × 100 / 20 × 100
Error: 0.000% (Perfect match!)

Example 3: Large Lead

Input: Lead = 100.0 mm, Pitch = 4.5 mm, Ratio = 40
Output: 20 × 80 / 30 × 60
Error: 0.016% (Excellent precision!)

🔧Troubleshooting

High Error Percentage

If the error percentage is higher than desired, try:

  • Adjusting the desired lead slightly
  • Double-checking your machine's exact leadscrew pitch
  • Verifying the dividing head ratio specification

No Valid Gear Combination Found

If no combination is found:

  • The requested lead may be impossible with your machine specs
  • Try a nearby lead value (±0.5 mm)
  • Contact your machine manufacturer for alternative gear sets
💡 Tip: Most calculations will find at least one solution within 0.1% error. Perfect matches (0% error) are common but not guaranteed for all lead values.

⭐Best Practices

  • Verify Machine Specs: Always confirm your machine's exact leadscrew pitch and dividing head ratio
  • Check Gear Availability: Before cutting, ensure all recommended gears are available in your workshop
  • Record Results: Save successful gear combinations for future reference
  • Test Cut: Make a test cut and measure the actual lead before full production
  • Gear Condition: Inspect gears for wear and damage before use
  • Proper Spacing: Ensure gears are properly spaced and meshed correctly
