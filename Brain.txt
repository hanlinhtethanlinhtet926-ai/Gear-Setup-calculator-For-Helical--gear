## 1. Helical Gear Setup Calculator

**Purpose**

Calculate optimal change gear configurations for manual milling machines.

**About**

Computes optimal change gear configurations by matching a target lead to available standard gear sets. Supports 19 languages and provides real-time error analysis.

Use this tool whenever the user asks about:

* Helical gear setup
* Change gears
* Lead calculations
* Milling machine gear configurations
* Gear combinations

How to Use the Helical Gear Calculator
Overview

This tool calculates the optimal change gear combinations for milling helical gears on a milling machine. You provide three machine parameters, and the calculator finds the best gear setups from a standard set of available gears. Login is required to access the calculator.

Step 1 — Sign In

You must be logged in before using the calculator. If you are not authenticated, the page will redirect you to the login screen automatically. Your logged-in email is shown in the header bar. Click Logout when you are done.

Step 2 — Select Language (Optional)

Use the language dropdown in the top-right corner to switch the interface language. Supported languages include English, Myanmar, Thai, Russian, Mongolian, Spanish, French, Chinese, Hindi, Dutch, Portuguese, Serbian, Ukrainian, Arabic, Japanese, Korean, German, Polish, and Norwegian.

Step 3 — Enter the Three Input Parameters
Input 1 — Desired Helix Length / Lead (L) in mm

Enter the helix lead you want to cut. This is the axial distance the helix advances in one full revolution. Example: 56.0
Input 2 — Leadscrew Pitch (P_L) in mm

Enter the pitch of your milling machine's leadscrew. This is the distance the table moves per one full revolution of the leadscrew. Example: 4.5
Input 3 — Dividing Head Ratio (R_DH)

Enter the gear ratio of your dividing head. Most standard dividing heads use a ratio of 40. Example: 40

Step 4 — Calculate

Click the Calculate Gears button. The calculator will compute the Machine Lead Constant (Cm = P_L × R_DH) and the Target Ratio (Rt = Cm / L), then search all combinations of standard gears to find the closest matches.
Standard gears used in the search:

20, 24, 28, 32, 36, 40, 44, 48, 52, 56, 60, 64, 72, 80, 84, 86, 90, 100, 120 teeth

Step 5 — Read the Results
The results panel shows:

Machine Lead Constant (Cm): the fixed drive constant of your machine setup
Target Ratio: the exact gear ratio needed to achieve your desired lead
Gear setup table showing four gears labeled A, B, C, and D:

Gear A — Driven 2 (connected to leadscrew side)
Gear B — Driven 1
Gear C — Driver 2 (connected to spindle/stud side)
Gear D — Driver 1


The gear arrangement formula is: (B × A) / (D × C)
Actual Ratio: the ratio produced by the selected gear combination
Actual Lead: the lead that will be cut with this gear setup (in mm)
Error: the percentage difference between the actual and target lead


Step 6 — Browse Alternative Setups

The calculator stores the top 50 closest gear combinations. Use the Next Setup and Previous Setup buttons to browse through them. The counter badge shows your current position (e.g., Setup 2 / 50). Use alternative setups if the best result has an error that is too large for your application or if you do not have one of the recommended gears available.

Notes

The calculator searches only standard even-numbered gears commonly available in change gear sets.
The first result shown is always the combination with the smallest error from the target ratio.
If all input values are valid but no results appear, check that your lead value is achievable with the available gear range.
Dark mode can be toggled using the moon/sun icon in the navigation bar. Your preference is saved automatically.

## 2. Gear Train Calculator

**Purpose**

Analyze multi-stage gear ratios, rotational speeds, and transmission efficiency.

**About**

Calculates:

* Gear ratios
* Output RPM
* Torque multiplication
* Pitch diameters
* Estimated transmission efficiency (98.5% per stage)

Use this tool whenever the user asks about:

* Gear trains
* Multi-stage transmissions
* Gear ratio calculations
* RPM calculations
* Torque calculations

How to Use the Helical Gear Train Calculator
Overview

This calculator computes gear train parameters for helical gear systems. It supports single-stage and two-stage gear trains. You enter the gear tooth counts, input speed, and gear geometry, and the calculator returns speed ratios, output speeds, and related gear metrics. Login is required to access the tool.

Step 1 — Sign In

You must be logged in before using the calculator. If you are not authenticated, the page will redirect you to the login screen automatically. Your logged-in email and current credit balance are shown in the header bar. Click Logout when you are done.

Step 2 — Enter Input Parameters
Input Speed (RPM)

Enter the rotational speed of the input shaft in revolutions per minute. Example: 1000
Pinion 1 Teeth

Enter the number of teeth on the first driving gear (pinion) in the first stage. Example: 20
Gear 1 Teeth

Enter the number of teeth on the first driven gear in the first stage. Example: 80
Pinion 2 Teeth (Optional)

Enter the number of teeth on the second driving gear (pinion) for a two-stage gear train. Leave blank if you only need a single-stage calculation. Example: 25
Gear 2 Teeth (Optional)

Enter the number of teeth on the second driven gear for the second stage. Leave blank for single-stage. Example: 75
Pressure Angle (degrees)

Enter the pressure angle of the gear teeth. The default value is 20 degrees, which is the most common standard. Change only if your gears use a different specification.
Helix Angle (degrees)

Enter the helix angle of the helical gear teeth. The default value is 15 degrees. Change to match your actual gear geometry.

Step 3 — Calculate

Click the Calculate button. Results will appear in the panel on the right side of the screen.

Step 4 — Read the Results
Results will display gear train parameters based on your inputs. For a single-stage train (only Stage 1 filled in), the calculator uses Pinion 1, Gear 1, the pressure angle, and the helix angle. For a two-stage train (all four gear fields filled in), the calculator chains both stages together and computes the overall output speed and combined ratio.

Notes

Pinion 2 and Gear 2 are both optional, but if you enter one you should enter the other to define a complete second stage.
The pressure angle and helix angle fields are pre-filled with standard defaults (20° and 15°). You only need to change them if your gear set uses different values.
Your credit balance is shown in the header. Each calculation may deduct credits depending on the site configuration.
Use the navigation bar to access the Dashboard, About page, or log out.

## 3. Bolt Torque Calculator

**Purpose**

Determine industrial tightening torque based on bolt specifications.

**About**

Calculates tightening torque using:

* Bolt dimensions
* Friction (K-factor)
* Required preload
* Engineering torque formulas

Use this tool whenever the user asks about:

* Bolt torque
* Tightening specifications
* Fasteners
* Torque calculations
* Mechanical assembly

How to Use the Industrial Bolt Torque Calculator
Overview

This calculator uses the standard nut-factor formula T = K × D × F to determine the recommended tightening torque for industrial bolts. Each calculation costs 1 credit.

Step 1 — Sign In

You must be signed in before using the calculator. If you are not logged in, the page will redirect you to the login screen automatically. Your current credit balance is shown in the top bar.

Step 2 — Choose a Preset (Optional)

Use the Standard Size Preset dropdown to auto-fill common bolt configurations. Available presets:

Metric Class 8.8: M6, M8, M10, M12, M16
Imperial SAE Grade 5: 1/4"-20, 3/8"-16, 1/2"-13, 5/8"-11, 3/4"-10

Selecting a preset will automatically set the unit system, diameter, and preload values.

Step 3 — Select Unit System

Choose Metric (mm / kN / N·m) or Imperial (inches / lbs / ft-lbs). Field labels and help text will update to match your selection.

Step 4 — Enter Bolt Diameter (D)

Enter the nominal outer diameter of the bolt shank. Use millimeters for metric or inches for imperial. Example: 12 for M12, or 0.5 for a 1/2" bolt.

Step 5 — Select Thread Condition

Choose the coating or lubrication condition that matches your fastener. The nut factor (K) will be filled in automatically based on your selection:

Standard Dry Steel / Unlubricated — K = 0.20
Lubricated Oil / Anti-Seize Paste — K = 0.15
Cadmium or Zinc-Plated — K = 0.22
Hot-Dip Galvanized — K = 0.25
Custom — enter your own K value manually


Step 6 — Enter Preload Force (F)

Enter the target clamping tension the bolt must achieve. Use kilonewtons (kN) for metric or pounds (lbs) for imperial. Example: 38.2 kN for an M12 bolt, or 5750 lbs for a 1/2" bolt.

Step 7 — Calculate

Click the Calculate Torque button. One credit will be deducted from your account. Results will appear on the right side showing:

Recommended tightening torque in your primary unit
The same torque converted to an alternative unit
Your input preload force for confirmation

Notes

You must have at least 1 credit to perform a calculation.
Preset values are based on Class 8.8 structural steel (metric) and SAE Grade 5 (imperial) standards.
If you select a preset, you can still manually adjust any value before calculating.
